# Siraat — Product Context

**Register:** product (the design serves the worship; it is never the spectacle).

## Product purpose
A focused, reverent, premium iOS companion for daily Muslim practice: accurate prayer
times + qibla, an offline Quran reader, recitation follow-along, the 99 Names, du'as,
tasbih, and live khutba translation. Offline-first and correct before it is anything else.
The app's job is to get out of the way of the worship.

## Users
Practicing Muslims who want a calm, dignified, premium reading and listening experience —
not a gamified, ad-cluttered, or "spiritual-wellness" pastel app. They notice when Arabic is
set badly, when an ayah is mis-attributed, or when a prayer time is wrong. They value:
- **Accuracy as sacred** — a wrong ayah, time, or attribution is disqualifying.
- **Calm focus** — one clear purpose per screen, generous quiet, nothing competing with scripture.
- **Works offline** — prayer, Quran, and reading must not depend on a signal.
- **Respect for attention** — no streaks, no nags, no upsell theater.

## Tone
Reverent, calm, confident, unobtrusive. Arabic is a first-class citizen, never decoration.
Warmth without sentimentality. The feeling of a well-made physical mushaf and a quiet masjid,
not a productivity app and not a meditation app.

## Anti-references (Siraat anti-slop — reject on sight)
- Mosque-silhouette-on-gradient hero images; crescent-moon / lantern clip art.
- Gold-on-emerald "Islamic clip-art" kitsch; ornate filigree borders used as decoration.
- Gamified streaks, badges, confetti, progress-guilt.
- Free-app ad clutter, interstitials, upsell modals.
- Generic AI-SaaS look: identical icon+heading+text card grids, the hero-metric template,
  decorative glassmorphism, gradient text, side-stripe accent borders.
- Pastel "wellness" palettes and rounded-blob illustration sets.
- Anything where ornament competes with or upstages the Arabic.

## Strategic principles
1. **Religious correctness is non-negotiable.** Never generate, alter, paraphrase, or guess
   Quranic text, translations, transliterations, attributions, or surah/ayah numbers. Design
   the container; the verified data fills it. (Mirrors the in-repo build script + bundled,
   attributed editions.)
2. **Reverence over decoration.** Every ornament must earn its place against the scripture
   beside it. When in doubt, remove it.
3. **Arabic typography is core**, not a font swap — Uthmani face, correct tashkeel, RTL,
   numerals, line breaking, and Dynamic Type scaling of the Arabic itself.
4. **Offline-first.** The reverent path works on a plane.
5. **One focus per screen.** A clear focal path and a single primary action.
6. **Accessibility is the floor**, not a feature: Dynamic Type (incl. Arabic), VoiceOver in
   the correct language, AA contrast, color-blind-safe status (color + symbol).
